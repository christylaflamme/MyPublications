# Publications page with citation charts

Goal: build a live web page of my published papers with charts of how my
citations have grown over the years, published so I can share it.

## Data

- ORCID: [0000-0002-0808-3475](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-0808-3475) — confirmed:
  Christy LaFlamme, Postdoctoral Scholar, Division of Medical Genetics, University
  of Washington.
- Source: OpenAlex, fetched via `fetch_papers.py` into `papers.json` (review copy
  in `papers_review.csv`).
- Cleanup applied (see `EXCLUDE_IDS` in `fetch_papers.py`, re-applied on every
  run): dropped 3 preprints whose published version is already in the list,
  2 duplicate RWTH Aachen repository entries for the same paper, 2 AACR
  conference abstracts restating listed papers, and a journal cover-art credit.
- Final count: 11 works (9 articles, 2 standalone preprints with no published
  version yet).
- **Standing rule**: when a preprint's published version lands in the list,
  move the preprint's citations onto the published paper (don't just drop
  them) before excluding the preprint. See `MERGE_PREPRINT_INTO_PUBLISHED` in
  `fetch_papers.py` — add a new pair there each time this comes up again.

## Design

- **Style**: clean & minimal — lots of white space, simple typography.
- **Accent color**: teal/green.
- **Color mode**: both light and dark, with a toggle.
- **Featured stats**: total papers, total citations (up top).
- **Charts**: citations per year (bar), cumulative citations (line), and
  papers published per year (bar) — three charts total.
- **Paper list**: title, venue, year; title links out to the DOI/landing page.
  No co-authors shown per paper.
- **Sort order**: newest first.
- **Intro**: short bio line under the name — "Postdoctoral Scholar, Division
  of Medical Genetics, University of Washington."
- **Extras**: link to ORCID profile near the name.
- **Interactivity**: hover tooltips on all charts showing exact values;
  a view toggle to switch the citations chart between per-year and cumulative;
  a filter toggle to include/exclude preprints from the stats and charts.
